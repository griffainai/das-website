What it costs

Every driver who leaves takes 8,000 to 20,000 dollars with them, and an empty seat until you refill it.

At scale, a few points of turnover is a six or seven-figure number. The good news: retention responds to things far cheaper than replacement. Recognition, a real first 90 days, recognized safety, and respect keep drivers for a fraction of what losing them costs.
