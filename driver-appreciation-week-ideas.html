National Truck Driver Appreciation Week 2026 runs September 13–19. Here are ideas fleets actually execute — pair one anchor gift with an event and public recognition. For dates, deadlines, and the full planning checklist, see the Driver Appreciation Week guide.

Ideas by effort

  • The anchor — a branded, gift-boxed appreciation kit for every driver.
  • Terminal events — catered breakfast or cookout at each location.
  • Public recognition — name safe-driving and million-mile achievers in comms and on social.
  • Leadership notes — a signed card from a real name, not a form email.
  • Milestone hand-offs — present years-of-service and safe-driving awards during the week.
  • Family-facing — a gift the OTR driver's family sees at home.

Ideas by fleet size

Under 25 drivers: a premium kit + a personal note from the owner. 25–100: add a terminal meal and public recognition. 100+: drop-ship kits to terminals, run events at each, and hand out milestone awards — see recognition programs to make it repeatable.
