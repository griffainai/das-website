The Retention Problem

The industry runs at 89% annual turnover. Recognition is the proven intervention.

Every fleet manager knows the number. 89% annual turnover among large OTR carriers (ATA). $8,000–$15,000 to replace each driver (ATRI). Most carriers try to solve it with pay increases. Pay helps — but it's rarely the determining factor in whether a driver stays or leaves.

Research consistently shows the leading driver of voluntary departure isn't compensation — it's feeling invisible. Feeling like a number in a dispatch system rather than a professional the company depends on. Driver Appreciation Week, held each September, is the industry's recognized moment to close that gap. Most carriers miss it or execute it poorly: a group pizza order, a generic email, a gift card that arrives three weeks late.

Driver Appreciation Solutions was built specifically to change that — with recognition that arrives on time, carries the fleet's brand, has the driver's name on it, and reflects the quality of the work they put in.
