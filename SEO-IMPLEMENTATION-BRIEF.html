2 Critical Design Constraint

The two new SEO pages MUST use the blog article layout — NOT the solution page layout.

The site has two layout types:
(A) Solution pages (solution-appreciation.html, etc.) — large hero sections, visual stat cards, icon grids, CTA banners with big imagery. Do not use this for the new SEO pages.

(B) Blog article pages (blog/cost-of-driver-turnover.html, etc.) — dark header with title + author, then a clean text-body article with prose, pull quotes, and stat boxes. Use this template for both new SEO pages.

How to copy the blog template

Open blog/cost-of-driver-turnover.html. Copy the entire file. Save as the new filename in the root directory (not inside blog/). Update all relative paths: change ../css/styles.css to css/styles.css, change ../contact.html to contact.html, etc. Then replace the content per Sections 3 and 4 below.

Article body content outline

Write ~1,600–2,000 words of informational prose. Follow this outline:

Opening paragraph (first 60 words — AI Overview bait, write this EXACTLY)

Commercial driver gifts are branded recognition items ordered in bulk by fleet operators, transportation companies, and logistics firms for their professional CDL drivers. Unlike consumer gift products, commercial driver programs are built for fleet procurement — priced per driver, shipped to a central distribution point, and designed around what a driver actually uses on the road.

H2: What Makes a Driver Gift Actually Work

2–3 paragraphs. The distinction between a real driver gift and a generic corporate catalog item repurposed for trucking. Key criteria: cab-friendly size, practical road use, personalization signal, leadership involvement (signed letter). Cite the concept that drivers notice when something was built for them vs. when "driver" was added to a catalog SKU.

H2: The 5 Occasions Fleets Use Driver Gift Programs

Short intro + a 5-item numbered list with a sentence each: (1) NTDAW, (2) New driver onboarding kits, (3) Safety milestones, (4) Tenure anniversaries, (5) Holiday season. Link each to the relevant solution page where applicable.

H2: Program Tiers and Per-Driver Cost

Use a .cost-breakdown table with 3 rows. Entry ($35–$50/driver): branded item + card. Standard ($55–$75/driver): 2–3 items + personalized certificate + leadership letter. Premium ($80–$95/driver): curated kit, full personalization, milestone inserts, dedicated account rep. Add: "For a 100-driver fleet, a Standard program runs $5,500–$7,500 — roughly half the cost of losing one driver."

H2: Ordering Process for Fleet Procurement

3–4 paragraphs or a numbered process list. Steps: (1) Submit driver count + occasion + target ship date, (2) Select kit tier, (3) Submit driver roster for personalization (optional), (4) Approve proof, (5) Production 5–10 days, (6) Ships to one address or drop-shipped by region. Note: PO and net-30 payment terms available for fleets with 50+ drivers.

H2: What to Avoid When Buying Driver Gifts

Short but sharp. Common mistakes: ordering too late (after Aug 7 for NTDAW), buying consumer products from Amazon and repackaging them, skipping the personalization/leadership letter, ordering a single size for branded apparel without collecting sizes, picking items that don't fit in a cab.
